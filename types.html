Types of Heart Disease

Understanding the different types of heart disease is crucial for early detection and proper treatment. Each type affects the heart in unique ways and may require specialized care.

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

Caused by plaque buildup in the coronary arteries. Often results in chest pain or heart attack.

Specialist: Cardiologist

Recommended Hospital: Mayo Clinic or Cleveland Clinic

Heart Failure

The heart is unable to pump blood effectively, leading to fluid buildup and fatigue.

Specialist: Heart Failure Specialist

Recommended Hospital: Mount Sinai Heart

Arrhythmias

Irregular heartbeats due to electrical signal issues.

Specialist: Electrophysiologist

Recommended Hospital: Johns Hopkins Hospital

Valvular Heart Disease

Damage or malfunction of heart valves, leading to blood flow problems.

Specialist: Cardiothoracic Surgeon

Recommended Hospital: Cleveland Clinic

Congenital Heart Defects

Heart abnormalities present at birth. May need surgery or intervention.

Specialist: Pediatric Cardiologist

Recommended Hospital: Boston Children's Hospital

Cardiomyopathy

Disease of the heart muscle that affects its ability to pump blood.

Specialist: Cardiologist

Recommended Hospital: Mayo Clinic

Pericarditis

Inflammation of the pericardium, often due to infection or autoimmune disorders.

Specialist: Cardiologist or Rheumatologist

Recommended Hospital: UCLA Medical Center

Rheumatic Heart Disease

Damage to heart valves from rheumatic fever, a complication of untreated strep throat.

Specialist: Infectious Disease Specialist & Cardiologist

Recommended Hospital: Apollo Hospitals (India)
